2016 Reddit Comments Visualization

     This project aims to visualize how various subreddits differ in comment volume, comment sentiment, and comment time distributions throughout the first 9 months of 2016. You will also find other variables that concern the nature of the comments. We utilized the publicly available Reddit comments datasets found on pushshift.io.

     There are a few different components to our visualization: an overview of subreddits, smaller visualizations that compare two subreddits in detail, and an extra visualization of how often subreddits mention each other. Relevant filters will be visible for the appropriate visualizations.

Left clicking or control+left clicking on subreddits in the main visualization will choose the first and second subreddits to compare, respectively. The comment subset filter allows for filtering on special subsets of the comments for a given subreddit:

  • All Comments: All the comments!
  • Top Score Comments: Only the top 5% of comments by score within each subreddit
  • Bottom Score Comments: Only the bottom 5% of comments by scoe within each subreddit
  • Most Popular Authors: Only the top 5% of authors by comment volume within each subreddit
  • Top Positive Comments: Only comments with a positive score of at least 25
  • Top Negative Comments: Only comments with a negative score of at least 15
  • Top Godwin Comments: Only comments with a positive Godwin score
  • Top Swear Comments: Only comments with a swear score of at least 35
